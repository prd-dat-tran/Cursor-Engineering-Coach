# Activity Patterns

The Patterns page reveals when, where, and how you work with Cursor.

## Work Hours

![Work Hours Heatmap](/screenshots/screen-patterns-workhours.png)

The Work Hours tab shows a heatmap of your Cursor activity across the week. Each cell represents an hour-long slot on a specific day of the week, and the color intensity indicates how many focused requests you sent during that time.

Below the heatmap, two charts compare weekday vs. weekend activity:

- **Hourly distribution** -- When during the day you are most active, split by weekday and weekend
- **Weekly volume** -- Total request counts per week, split by weekday and weekend

These patterns help you identify your peak focus hours and understand your work-life balance with Cursor.

## Calendar

The Calendar tab presents a GitHub-style contribution grid. Each day is a cell colored by activity level. This gives you a long-range view of consistency and streaks.

## Projects

![Projects View](/screenshots/screen-patterns-projects.png)

The Projects tab breaks down activity by workspace. For each project, you see:

- **Estimated hours** of Cursor-assisted development
- **Total requests** sent
- **Estimated lines of code** generated
- **Work pattern** (e.g., "mostly weekdays", "mostly weekends")
- **Tech stack** tags detected from the files you worked on
- **Hot files** -- The files most frequently referenced in your Cursor conversations
- **Activity summary** table with key metrics

This view is valuable for understanding which projects consume the most Cursor resources and how your usage patterns differ across codebases.
